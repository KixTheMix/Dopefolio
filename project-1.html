The Shoe (2026) PC

A passion project bite-size hybrid visual novel about losing your shoe at a party.

Made by 2 people over the summer of 2025 + January 2026 using Unity, Adobe Photoshop & Yarnspinner.

Download Game

Project Description

The Shoe puts the player in quite the situation. You're drunk at a houseparty in the middle of the winter and as you leave you realize one small detail... someone has stolen your other Matsune Hiku shoe! But as you search the house and to confront the thief you realize that your perception of the world has been twisted.

This game was a passion project by me and my significant other, I was the Writer/Narrative Designer while he was the Game Designer/Artist/Programmer. We wanted to make a fun and short game, and the idea stayed an idea until the summer of 2025 when it began development. We finished the game in January 2026 after putting it on the back-burner since September 2025.

Writing

The challenge I set for myself was to make something anxiety inducing into something comedic.

The Goals
- I wanted the story to be immersive to the player where the added visual element would only enhance it.
- There needed to be sillyness sprinkled throughout the story as it wasn't supposed to be serious.
- The feeling of being drunk was essential to the story and would tie in with the comedy. It would also further the immersion for the player. It would also act as a struggle for the player as the character would be unable to see faces.
- A hook was needed to interest and engage the player in the start, but the player also had to be motivated to follow the character throughout the whole game.

Immersion
- The Shoe was the main problem in the story and acted as hook.
- The character you play has is a couple of drinks into the night, and it needed to present and obvious to the player without the game telling them that. It needed to be conveyed through the game and not simply through text.
- The internal dialogue included both descriptions of the characters environment and the characters feelings and perception of everything. Comments and remarks about the things transpiring around the player.

Visual impairment
- The faces of all characters within the games are blurry and is the main reason behind that was to make it harder for the player to identify the thief. They instead have to rely on other physical descriptions given by other characters.
- Characters stand out visually from each other and this is done through graphics and physical descriptions through both internal thoughts and conversation.
- All environmental descriptions are also described but follow the same format. They're filled with comments from the drunk player and a general feeling of disconnect.

Characters
- All the characters have different personalities that affect how they interact with the player, giving both a more interesting experience and a feeling of believability into the world.
- The characters goal is also to help the player by giving them clues about the thief and the reality around them.
- Some characters are more helpful towards the player while some act to fill out the world and exist in the background or included in an activity. As the game is set in a houseparty filled with people, it was important to make sure it did not feel empty.

Player choices

As usual in Visual Novels, the player has the agency to make their own choices to influence the story as well as to create their own path and order of things.

Narrative Choices
- The player always has a choice of how to approach a new character or how to respond to them.
- The purpose if to give the player a sense of control and immersion with the world and the character they inhabit. But they have no real effect on the gameplay itself.

Gameplay Choices
- The player can control how much more or less drunk the character is throughout the game.
- This is done through situations where the player can choose to drink more alcohol, or take it easy and sober up. This is shown through the wobbly effect on the screen and it's severity.

Level Design

Even though the game was in a 2d world, some level design had to be made to counteract a disconnect between the gameworld and the player.

The rooms
- The placements of the room was to mimic a normal house layout.
- The lower floor contained the most people and interactions with other characters. This is also where the clues of the thief are found.
- The upper floors were meant to be more calm and give more introspection for the character you play as. More environmental interactions reside here and slow the pace down, to give the character time to breathe.
- At first the player needed to visit all the rooms to progress but this was removed due to lack of reason and motivation.
- The purpose of the rooms were to further immerse the player. But also to diversify the experience with different interactions and paces.
