HALLOSTEAL (2026) PC

You are a child on Halloween and you need some candy.

Made in two days with three other people.

Download Game

Project description

Hallosteal is a fast-paced rougelite that was made by me as the level designer and 3 other people. The gameplay features the player going through levels to collect candy as currency by attacking other characters. The levels are randomized and are different sizes in varying difficulties.

Level Design

The goal was to create different levels that allowed the player to race around utilize the movement and levels layout to reach their goal. The levels needed to allow for seamless movement.

Smaller levels

There were two smaller levels in the game including the tutorial level, that teaches the player how the game works.



What and why
- These levels were small -> limited area to move around.
- Have obstacles -> for the player to test movement and layouts.
- No dead ends -> flowing movement.
- Objects to jump on to early introduce that concept for later levels when it is more relevant.

Medium levels

There were four medium levels that lets the player move around in and explore a bigger area.



What and why
- These levels were bigger -> more area to explore.
- More obstacles -> even more paths for the player.
- Sections -> cannot see the whole map at once -> incentive to explore.
- More places for the player to stand on.

Big levels

There were two big levels .



What and why
- Dead ends with alternate ways to move -> encourage the player to platform around.
- Sections were later colour coded -> reduce chance of getting lost.
- Areas to circle around -> useful for when escaping enemies.
- Multiple rooms -> more sections to run around.
